Certificate Authorities

This panel is only available in Extended View.

To get to this panel, press button Settings in JonDo's main window, then choose the entry Certificate Authorities from the list to the left.

A certificate is a (digital) container for a public encryption key, digitally signed by a certification authority (e.g. the JonDo team), that approves for third parties by the signature that this key belongs to a given person or organisation. If this approval for certificates didn't exist, anyone could pretend to be the JonDo team or a trusted Mix operator, and may operate honypot Mixes, for example, or may engage in the communication between users and really trusted Mixes/operators and read all data. Moreover, the insertion of faked update messages would be easy. (Further infromation concerning certificates...)

  • I only want to use servers certified by the following authorities:
    This list contains all certification authorities that you trust.

  • Import...
    With this button, you can add certification authorities to the list of trusted authorities. The appropriate certificate should be chosen in the dialog field that appears.

  • Remove
    This button removes an entry from the list.

  • Disable
    By clicking this button, you can deactivate an entry without removing it from the list.

  • Details...
    Click on this button to the the certificate of the selected entry.
