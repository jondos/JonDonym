JonDonym, AN.ON and Tor

The anonymization service JonDonym, a technical branch from the AN.ON Project, consists of multiple user selectable mix cascades. A cascade itself consists of three mix servers operated by independent and non interrelated organizations or private individuals who all publish their identity. The operators have to abide by strict provisions which prohibit saving connection data or exchanging such data with other operators in a cascade. Every connection from a user is re-encrypted by every mix server within a cascade and transferred through the cascade to the target, e.g. a website. The aim of multiple encryption and processing is to make sure no mix operator can by himself alone expose the user, and that eavesdropping on the connection (Internet provider, WLAN) produces zero useful content. Also, since a lot of users surf the anonymization service simultaneously, and thus share the same IP address, all connections of every user are concealed amidst each other: any user could have been the source of a certain connection. This procedure ensures that no mix operator or external party (Internet provider, WLAN sniffers, websites) can discover who has initiated a certain connection or what connections a certain user has made.

JonDonym has some very specific advantages to other anonymization services:

  • Strong encryption and authentication: Nobody can read the traffic between your PC and the JonDonym service.
  • Protection from the mix operator: Unless all mix operators in a JonDonym cascade are forced separately to monitor you, your internet connections are safe. The mix operators are revaluated regularly concerning seriosity and professionalism. Their identity is made public also. Even in the highly improbable case that they all are forced to unmask a certain connection, this function would have to be compiled into each server software first.
  • No logfiles: Saving any logfiles with IP addresses, even for seconds, is forbidden to the mix operators by contract.
  • Transparent functionality: Informative GUI, published concepts and open, OSI compliant sourcecode of all software components.
  • No software backdoor: There is no possibility to inspect single connections in the JonDo client. The software runs completely on your PC and can not be manipulated without your approval. No identity, serial number or account number is transmitted during update.
  • Secure browser profile: With JonDoFox you are given a Firefox profile for free which has been especially developed for anonymous and secure surfing with JonDonym.
  • Portable anonymity: JonDo runs without installation on the PC directly from a portable USB stick (Windows / Mac OS X).
  • Evolution by research: Scientific work conducted at the Technical University of Dresden (AN.ON), the University of Regensburg (AN.ON) and at the JonDos GmbH (JonDonym) enhances and improves the system.
  • Competent, fast and at no charge: Detailed help, monitored user forum, email support.

A similar standard of anonymization can only be offered by the non-commercial system Tor, which we recommend to advanced Internet users in addition to JonDonym. Internet beginners and computer novices should be careful with Tor: there is no control over who runs the servers in this service over which your data travels. In the past, there has been ongoing suspicion that criminals and intelligence agencies exploit the Tor network in order to secretly attain unencrypted information like passwords, bank accounts and credit cards. Certain tricks can enable the Tor server to concentrate much of the traffic on itself, making the exploit even more effective. Tor may be set as proxy in JonDo though, making surfing slower but even more secure than with JonDonym alone because the last servers in this chain will be part of the verified JonDonym network.

But as a matter of fact, every last mix server in a JonDonym cascade could record data which is not separately secured by HTTPS/SSL. Unlike Tor and other services, in JonDonym only seriosity certified and publicized persons and organizations may operate a mix server. The user may then decide for himself on whether he trusts the operator or not. All this makes it highly improbable for criminal operators to infiltrate JonDonym and go about unpunished.

Premium services

With JonDonym you can choose between free services and the premium services which are subject to a charge. By this voluntary prepaid payment for premium services you support the preservation and improvement of the JonDonym system. The premium services offer these additional advantages to the basic JonDonym:

  • Guaranteed performance: Speed of at least 100 kbit/s and latencies of 4 seconds maximum during 90% of the day. Free services, also Tor, are usually much slower.
  • Guaranteed access: A connection to the JonDonym system is guaranteed all the time.
  • Legal certainty: Development, support and billing addresses are all located in Germany and not at some anonymous offshore company without a summonable address. Germany sets worldwide standards in customer protection.
  • Three-Operator-Guarantee: Every mix cascade is run by three organizations or persons that are independent from each other.
  • Guaranteed internationality: Mixes and operators of a cascade are spread over at least two countries. This ensures that no large network node (CIX) can monitor all data traffic. And it cripples every national data retention since not all mixes in a cascade do log.
  • Guaranteed connection: The mix operators do not block any Internet ports.
  • Soon: SOCKS support: You can use the SOCKS protocol aside HTTP to anonymize most Internet applications.

If you would like to use premium services, create a disposable account and activate it by an anonymous or non anonymous payment of your choice. After that you will have a certain amount of data volume at your disposal, depending on your chosen rate, to be used with the premium services. Once it is depleted, you can create and activate another disposable account any time.

Advice on protection of personal information: for accurate clearing of data volumes with the mix operators it is saved how much volume an account has used cumulatively on a mix cascade. These clearings happen no sooner than on a daily basis. An allocation of accounts to user identities is not saved. It is technically impossible to recognize requested websites by the deducted data volumes alone, even when monitoring accurately to the second (maximum accuracy).
