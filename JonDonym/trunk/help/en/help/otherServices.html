VPN services and Proxies

Additional safety with VPN services like Relakks, Steganos Anonym VPN, Perfect Privacy, Cyber Ghost VPN, XeroBank VPN

JonDonym and Tor may be the best anonymization systems technically, but both share small disadvantages from concept that should be considered when used:

  • Browser plugins (Java/Flash/Silverlight/ActiveX) must be deactivated. Otherwise, your PC's/router's true IP can be revealed by a website.
  • In principle, transfer speed will be clearly below ADSL capacity (maximum of 1-2 MBit/s possible).
  • Applications are not transparently anonymized but each has to be configured (the JonDoFox browser is preconfigured though).
  • Not all applications can be anonymized (HTTP/SOCKS proxies must be supported by the application).

If JonDo is not compatible with a certain application, or if you wish for less strong anonymization in favor of speed, it can be helpful to use a VPN (Virtual Private Network) system at the side of JonDonym as an additional layer of security. A VPN can at least offer a limited protection to your IP address if you use (unsigned) Java or Flash applets from time to time. But in this case, you should take care that your computer is behind a router or running in a virtual machine. JonDos is preparing its own supplementary VPN offer which will be integrated in JonDo as a combi-package until the end of 2009.

For web surfing, except bigger downloads maybe, VPN services should not be used. On one hand, their hosts usually do not ensure that users also have an uniform appearance on the Web aside their IP address (see last chapter). And on the other hand, a local observer on your network (ISP, WLAN) could guesstimate websites requested over VPN quite well by analyzing size and timing of the encrypted VPN data stream. JonDonym and Tor are quite resilient against this attack (a scientific article which demonstrates the attack and lists experimental results is in preparation; the success rates are over 90% for VPNs). You should also keep in mind that VPN hosts can, unlike JonDonym and Tor, track and save every step of yours since they control all servers in the VPN. Nevertheless, protection by the VPN of a professional and reputable host is most of the time better than no protection at all.

Network proxies and web proxies

For this kind of "anonymization on demand" proxy services are particularly famous besides the already mentioned services. Proxies are literally proxy PCs which take upon communication between your PC and the Internet, relay it to the target and send the answer back to your PC. Huge lists of the so-called network proxies or "anonymous" proxies are offered for free download on the Internet and can be set in every browser without a hassle. Some software providers distribute programs with which these lists can be updated automatically. In addition to these proxies, there are webproxy services, Internet pages with a form field, in which the user can input the target address that he want's to visit anonymously. The webproxy subsequently delivers the content of the requested website and automatically patches all links to again use the webproxy when clicked. For using webproxy services the browser configuration does not have to be changed.

Apart from compatibility problems of webproxies with JavaScript (JavaScript URLs can often not be substituted correctly), proxies are commonly unsafe: many proxies are PCs hijacked by hackers or criminals. Some give your IP away to the requested webserver. Connections with proxies are almost always unencrypted, so that an eavesdropper on your connection could observe your surfing behavior. Moreover, the proxy operator can, of course, watch exactly what you are doing. Proxies offer thus, if at all, only some protection from the website's host but not from third parties.

Local proxies

The program JonDo works as a local proxy directly on your PC. Just as with normal proxies your browser (or other applications on your PC configured for JonDo resp.) has to connect to it in order to access the Internet indirectly. But since JonDo runs on your own PC, instead of on a third party server, it is not affected by the generic proxy security flaws mentioned above. JonDo itself relays communication of your Internet applications over a multi-encrypted, direct TCP connection to a JonDonym mix cascade.
